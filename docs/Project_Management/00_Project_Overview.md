# 1. Introduction

The Office Asset, Equipment Reservation, Borrowing, and Inventory Management System is a centralized digital platform designed for the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) Region XII.

The system aims to improve the management of office assets, equipment reservations, borrowing transactions, returns, and consumable inventory by replacing manual paper-based recording with a secure and traceable digital solution.

The system will provide accurate asset tracking, improve accountability, simplify inventory monitoring, and generate management reports for authorized personnel.

---

# 2. Project Vision

To develop a secure, reliable, and scalable asset management platform that promotes accountability, improves operational efficiency, and supports the digital transformation of PSA Region XII.

---

# 3. Problem Statement

The current asset management process relies heavily on manual recording and paper-based documentation.

Common challenges include:

- Borrowed equipment is manually recorded or sometimes not recorded at all.
- Difficulty identifying the current borrower of an asset.
- Missing or inconsistent return records.
- Manual inventory monitoring.
- Difficult retrieval of historical transaction records.
- Time-consuming report generation.
- Increased risk of lost or misplaced equipment.
- Limited audit trail for accountability.

---

# 4. Project Objectives

The system aims to:

- Digitize asset and inventory management.
- Maintain complete transaction history.
- Improve accountability through QR Code tracking.
- Generate printable borrowing and return receipts.
- Generate inventory and management reports.
- Reduce manual paperwork.
- Track borrowing and return dates.
- Provide secure role-based access control.
- Improve operational efficiency.

# 10. Assumptions

- Every borrowable asset will receive a unique QR Code.
- Users have authorized PSA accounts.
- Internet connection is available within the office.
- Only authorized personnel can perform administrative actions.
- Existing paper records can be migrated into the system.
